# SpaceX rachète Cursor pour $60b?

## Episode Summary

In this news-packed episode, the hosts dive into the shocking $60 billion acquisition offer from SpaceX for Cursor, exploring Elon Musk's strategy to dominate AI compute. They discuss Anthropic's strategic moves including Cloud Design, OpenAI's legal battles, the explosion of open-source models, and the evolving role of data teams in an AI-first world. The conversation covers everything from GPU wars to the gamification of software development.

## Key Topics Discussed

- **SpaceX's $60B offer for Cursor** - Elon Musk's strategy to control AI compute through vertical integration
- **Anthropic's expansion** - Recruiting CTOs from major tech companies, launching Cloud Design, and limiting API access
- **OpenAI vs. Elon Musk lawsuit** - Legal battle over OpenAI's transition from non-profit to for-profit
- **Open source model explosion** - QwQ, DeepSeek's 1.6 trillion parameter model competing with Opus and GPT-4.5
- **Google's $70B investment in Anthropic** - $40B upfront plus $30B in milestones, including TPU access
- **Cloud Design release** - Anthropic's Figma competitor showcasing chat-first interface philosophy
- **Dynamic software interfaces** - YC's call for startups building AI-personalized user experiences
- **The future of data teams** - Shift from coding dashboards to writing agent guidelines
- **Agent Craft** - RTS game-style interface for managing software development agents

## Notable Quotes

> "Imagine if we had said 10 years ago that a company who makes spaceships would buy a company who makes an IDE... and that IDE is just a fork of Microsoft's IDE."

> "The problem with skills is that now everyone wants to have a skill for everything... people love having lots of skills, it gives the impression of having control, even though the information could be much more compact."

> "Between now and a year ago, for the same task I don't think quality changed much. But what really changed is the runtime - today you can tell an agent 'run until fixed' and it will actually do it."
